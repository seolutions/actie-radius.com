What is Human Growth Hormone (HGH)?

Human Growth Hormone is a hormone naturally produced by the human body from the pituitary gland. It supports the immune system, speeds up metabolism, helps repair damaged cells, and speeds up cell division, which is part of the body's process to create new cells. With age, the body's natural production of this hormone will decrease, eventually leading to a condition called Adult Onset Growth Hormone Deficiency. This can be characterized by loss of energy, weight gain, loss of muscle mass, anxiety, stress, interrupted sleep cycle, and joint pain. A lack of HGH can also contribute to diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension. Low energy levels and loss of lean muscle mass can be a serious impediment for bodybuilders, athletes, and fitness enthusiasts in general.
